Redesign on Sydney's Press Page

Sydney's Press Page

For this project, I chose to redesign the press page of a local Cafe in Providence, which is sydney.

Sydney is my favorite Cafe in Providence! I study at sydney and love to spend time with my friends there. Unfortunately, it's temporarily closed due to the Pandemic. Hope you could stop by and try their incredible Matcha Latte when it’s reopened.

Usability Problem
  • The news titles under the section press clippings looks like a plain text instead of a clickable text
  • The news titles are not eye-catching
  • The display of the embedded video is not straightforward. Hard to tell there's a video in the web page (see the screenshot of 70% web view).
    • Learnability Problem
  • In the mobile version, “menu” icon on the top of sydney is confusing: it could refer to the menu of the website or the menu of the Cafe
  • In fact, the "menu" on the left image refers to the menu of the website, and the first "menu" on the right image refers to the menu of the Cafe
    • Memorability Problem
  • In the mobile version, “menu” icon on the top of sydney may cause potential memorability issues: “menu” should contain the following sub-categories: home, food menu, baked goods, press, locations, gift cards instead of displaying menu with those sub-categories all together. Users may forget how to navigate the page when they leave the page and then return to it (See the images above).
    • Accessibility Problem

    I used WAVE and find some potential accessibility issues listed below:

  • When users hover/click on the "press" button in the menu, the button change its color from black to light pink to indicate users' action. The button is also in light pink if the users are currently browsing the "press" page. However, WAVE suggests the color contrast between the light pink text and the white background is low.
  • There's no alternative text for the picture showcasing the Cafe in the web page.
  • When I use WAVE to see how would a screen reader read the web page, I found, for the customer’s review section, the screen reader only reads the name of the reviewer but not their review to the cafe.
    • Visual Redesign

    To solve the problem mentioned above, I made the following changes:

    1. Add underlines to the news titles to indicate they are clickable In addition, when users hover over the links, the color of the title change to light pink.
    2. Make the video embedded in the web page. Show the cover of the video so that users know there's a video in the web page.
    3. Use a nav bar to represent the menu of the website. In this case, the "menu" in the page now only represents the menu of the Cafe. Make the nav bar responsive. This would also solve the hierarchy issue mentioned in the Memorability Problem section.
    4. Add an alternative text to the image howcasing the Cafe in the web page.
    5. Make the color contrast between the buttons and the background bigger (see high-fi prototypes for more details).
